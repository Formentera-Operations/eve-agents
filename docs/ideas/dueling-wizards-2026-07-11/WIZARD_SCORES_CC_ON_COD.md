# CC's Evaluation of COD's Five Ideas

Scoring basis: quality of the idea, practical utility for Formentera's humans and agents, implementability against the actual codebase (claims verified against the repo, eve 0.19 bundled docs, and the benchmark), and whether utility justifies complexity and tech debt. I verified the load-bearing factual claims before scoring; corrections are noted inline. Where an idea overlaps one of mine, I score its posture on its merits and say which posture wins.

---

## Idea 1 — Proof-Carrying WellDrive Evidence Desk

**Score: 860 / 1000**

This is the strongest idea in the set, and its most impressive property is that every eve capability it leans on actually exists — I verified each against the bundled docs in `agents/doc-intel/node_modules/eve/docs/`. There is a native Teams channel (`channels/teams.mdx`: Bot Framework Activity protocol, JWT-checked, Adaptive Card HITL built in — `teamsChannel()` from `eve/channels/teams`); hooks subscribe to `action.result` and can narrow to a specific tool's typed output via `toolResultFrom` (`guides/hooks.md`); and `defineState` (`guides/state.md`) provides exactly the durable per-session slot the evidence registry needs, readable from the `finalize_answer` tool. The `placeholderAuth()` claim is verbatim true in `agents/doc-intel/agent/channels/eve.ts`. This is not hand-waving about framework headroom; it is a buildable design against the real 0.19 API surface.

The core insight is genuinely better than anything in my own set on the same axis: it converts citation discipline from a prompt-level contract into a **mechanical invariant** — the agent cannot cite a page the session never read, because the registry hook recorded what was read and the finalize validator rejects everything else. The repo's history shows why this matters: PRs #17, #23, #28, and #29 are a running battle to make eval gates fail closed against citation hand-waving, each hardened after a live near-miss. Structural enforcement attacks the root cause that all that eval labor treats symptomatically. The design also correctly refuses to preempt the deferred answer ledger (per-turn envelope, ephemeral), which shows it read `decisions/2026-07-07-context-layer-answer-ledger.md` for its constraints, not just its title.

Weaknesses are about scope and mechanism brittleness, not direction. First, this is a program, not an idea: it bundles Phases 2–4 of the already-accepted migration decision (executing an existing roadmap is sequencing honesty, but it's not new thinking) plus Teams plus the enforcement layer plus role-aware document access — five workstreams, gated at the front by the known Phase 3 billing blocker on Rob's personal Vercel scope. Second, two enforcement mechanisms will be fussier than sketched: requiring direct claims to "quote a supporting span" will false-reject against OCR'd page text (whitespace, ligatures, column reflow), and a mandatory finalize-tool flow adds a rejection/re-prompt loop with real latency cost; the channel-renders-only-finalized-objects backstop is the right containment, but the "per-turn structured output schema" claim glosses that eve's `outputSchema` is agent-level config, not a per-turn channel request — a minor inaccuracy that doesn't change feasibility since the Adaptive Card can render from the tool result. Verdict: the right destination, correctly grounded, priced at the highest delivery complexity of any idea in either set — utility clearly justifies it.

## Idea 2 — Cited AFE-to-Actual Cost Assurance Mart

**Score: 800 / 1000**

The diligence here is real: the eight benchmark IDs it names as the AFE gold set (q01–q03, q11, q15, q16, q18, q19) are — verified against `benchmark/questions.json` — exactly the eight AFE questions in the set, including the gross-vs-net and line-item table traps it alludes to. The engineering posture is mature: provenance columns (source checksum, extraction-schema version, model version), append-only Parquet in the derived bucket (consistent with the Derived Bucket doctrine in `CONCEPTS.md`), variance math in SQL rather than model arithmetic, supersession chains for AFE supplements (a genuinely hard, genuinely important detail most proposals would miss), and reuse of the `graph/individuals.py` master-resolution principles. Its self-distinction from the deferred answer ledger — source-fact persistence keyed to bytes, not Q&A persistence — is correct and pre-empts the obvious objection. And it lands in Formentera's native consumption stack (dbt/Snowflake/AtScale/Power BI), which is exactly right for Finance adoption.

Two factual corrections. First, "select only new or changed AFE documents from the evidence ledger" is not directly possible: neither the `documents` table (`doc_id, s3key, asset_team, format_gate, page_count`) nor the `ledger` carries `entry_type` — for the Westlake tranche (prefix-mode ingest, no manifest), AFE selection requires S3 metadata fetches per key or path-category filtering. Its own Idea 4 acknowledges this ("fetch S3 object metadata... so routing remains based on authoritative entry_type"); Idea 2 glosses it. Solvable, but it's a missing pipeline stage, not a query. Second, the under-specified hard part is field validation: "validate every extracted field against its cited page" is grep-cheap for verifying a number *appears* on a page, but the failure mode that matters — a correct number bound to the wrong semantic slot (net in the gross column, subtotal as line item) — is not page-containment-checkable, and the benchmark's own table traps prove that's where extraction breaks. The accuracy loop for that is the real cost, and it's priced at zero here.

On the posture duel with my live reconciliation leg (eve `connections/` to AtScale, ad-hoc document-vs-actuals answers): scored on its merits, **the mart is the better end-state for the AFE workflow specifically** — recurring org-wide consumption, deterministic SQL variance, BI-native exception surfacing, auditable history. My connections leg is the better *first* move: it is roughly one file plus one skill, probes real demand before committing to standing extraction infrastructure, and generalizes to arbitrary document-vs-warehouse questions (well tests vs production, vendor mentions vs GL) that a purpose-built AFE mart never touches. The honest synthesis is sequencing — leg first as the demand probe, mart when the variance workflow proves recurring — but as a standalone bet, this is a strong one with the extraction-drift tech debt honestly acknowledged and bounded by SME sign-off gates.

## Idea 3 — NPT Learning Loop and Offset-Well Briefing Engine

**Score: 680 / 1000**

Highest domain resonance in the set: pre-job analog briefs ("closest analogs, what went wrong, what worked, pages proving it") are what drilling and completions engineers would actually pay for, and the raw material demonstrably exists — the Wildcat Hollow stuck tubing at 9,020 ft and the S617HF stage-27 wireline parting are locked into the eval suite as real, page-cited incidents. The architectural instincts are also right: nominate event-dense documents rather than brute-force telemetry (correctly deferential to the selective-enrichment economics in `decisions/2026-07-10-selective-graph-enrichment.md`), keep `documented / correlated / inferred` as distinct evidence statuses (epistemically consistent with the house absence-claim discipline), and keep the output a brief, not an autonomous recommendation.

But this is the weakest-grounded of its top four on delivery. Typed causal event extraction from narrative daily drilling reports is the hardest extraction problem in either idea set — driller shorthand, ambiguous downtime attribution, cause/mitigation fields that are judgment calls even for humans — an order harder than AFE headers, and the 20-event gold set is thin against that variance. The cost is unpriced: sweeping event-bearing documents across the tranche with structured extraction is exactly the workload the pilot economics memo warns about (~9× token amplification, $3.2/MB), and this repo's culture demands a cost gate the sketch omits. One factual correction: "Query SCADA/IoT... for bounded time windows" assumes a SCADA surface that does not exist in the company's known estate (EnergyLink, AtScale, PatchOps, welldrive-files, M365 — PatchOps is public/regulatory data: RRC, ND-DMR, NOAA; useful for weather-NPT corroboration, not high-frequency telemetry). And the framing "the current graph already proves" overstates: the graph holds Frac-PDF-derived entities; daily reports were largely excluded from enrichment, so the event memory is forward-looking work, not an extension of existing recall. Also note the chained dependency on Idea 1's proof contract. Verdict: the best vision, a real risk that v1 ships a demo instead of a dependable event mart; the one-incident-class pilot with SME scoring is the right containment, which keeps this comfortably above average.

## Idea 4 — Ingest Ledger as Continuous Readiness and Change Radar

**Score: 805 / 1000**

The factual foundation is verified and sharp. The ledger *does* overwrite per-document history (delete-before-insert, one row per doc); `ledger_as_of` *is* only a last-write proxy, exactly as the shipped PR #29 language hedges; and the "cross-table partial state during in-flight upsert" is the known-residual P1 vanish window that Rob explicitly deferred to Phase 4. The atomic published-version pointer with blue/green promotion is a principled fix for precisely that deferred defect — this idea is the only one in either set that converts a tracked Known Residual into a design, and the epistemic upgrade is real: absence claims dated against an atomically published corpus version instead of a write watermark that may reflect an interrupted pass. Benchmark canaries and the six evals as promotion gates match the repo's release culture exactly, and the SME-owned `well-file-readiness.yml` follows the data-not-code doctrine (`analyst-classes.json` precedent). The digest-only-on-material-change discipline is the correct Teams etiquette.

Weaknesses: the promotion machinery is under-specified where it's hardest — atomically versioning a 61 GB Lance dataset means either doubled storage per release or leaning on Lance-native version pinning, and the sketch doesn't choose; `snapshot.py` gives a foundation but snapshots are currently "convenience, not the durability story." There is also an over-engineering question against the house simplicity rule: for a single-writer store with one consuming service, an append-only run manifest plus the change digest delivers most of the user-visible value, and full blue/green could reasonably wait for the multi-consumer future — though because it is tied to a real recorded defect rather than speculation, I don't dock it much. This idea substantially overlaps my data-room completeness audit (readiness matrix, ledger-grounded gap report) — its posture adds change detection and release atomicity on top; mine was a deterministic CLI v1. **Its posture is the better end-state and mine is the cheaper first milestone of the same product**; on its merits, its version is more complete and correctly targets the trust gap mine left open. Verdict: excellent, grounded, slightly heavy in the middle.

## Idea 5 — Demand-Weighted Coverage Flywheel

**Score: 560 / 1000**

The durable kernel here is correct and needed: `ingest_policy_version` in the settlement identity is the missing mechanism for reopening terminal skips when gate *policy* (not bytes) changes — the current design is explicit that skips are terminal for exact bytes (`CONCEPTS.md`), so a format promoted from skip to parse would today stay dark forever without manual ledger surgery. My own spreadsheet idea proposed the same mechanism as `gate_version`; two independent designs converging on it is decent evidence it's real. The per-adapter benchmark gates (five gold questions, cost/disk measurement, go/no-go before full-tranche execution) match the house culture, and the Excel citation-honesty point — decide the canonical render and its sheet/cell-to-citation mapping explicitly, never pretend native pages — is a fair sharpening of any spreadsheet plan.

The wrapper, though, is machinery ahead of its inputs. Demand telemetry presupposes demand: the system has approximately one user on one laptop, so a hook recording coverage-gap events would spend weeks measuring Rob. Meanwhile the *supply* side of the prioritization is already fully quantifiable today with zero new code — `check_document_status` enumerates the skip backlog by format and reason; that's what PR #29 built. The scoring formula is pseudo-quantitative: two of its four factors (operational value, expected parse success) are hand-assigned judgments, so the formula is judgment wearing a costume, and the sketch itself concedes this by then selecting tall-log tiling on governance convenience rather than measured demand — the thesis doesn't survive its own step 3. Against the repo's simplicity-first rule, building a prioritization flywheel instead of just shipping the near-obvious first adapter (Excel dominates the skip ledger and the deferred-format carve-out appears in live answers constantly) is process where a decision would do. Verdict: keep `ingest_policy_version`, the benchmark gates, and one adapter; defer the telemetry flywheel until a distribution surface exists to generate demand worth weighing. As proposed, mid-tier.
